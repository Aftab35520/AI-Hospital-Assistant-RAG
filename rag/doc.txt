Apollo Hospitals, Greams Road, Chennai – A Comprehensive Profile
Overview and Founding Legacy
Apollo Hospitals, Greams Road, Chennai, stands as a landmark institution in Indian healthcare. As the flagship hospital of the Apollo Hospitals Group, it was established in 1983 by Dr. Prathap C. Reddy, a visionary cardiologist who pioneered the concept of corporate healthcare in India . At a time when India's healthcare landscape was dominated by trust-run missionary hospitals and understaffed government facilities, Dr. Reddy envisioned a world-class, multispecialty hospital equipped with imported technology and highly trained medical professionals . The 150-bed hospital that opened on Greams Road revolutionized the sector by offering advanced medical care at a fraction of the cost of international treatment. Heart surgeries that cost upwards of $50,000 in the United States were made available for approximately $5,000 at Apollo, fundamentally changing how Indians accessed complex medical procedures .

Today, the hospital serves as the cornerstone of an expansive healthcare network that encompasses over 70 hospitals, more than 10,000 beds, and 400 clinics across India and internationally . The institution has grown from its original 150-bed capacity to a comprehensive medical campus offering over 60 specialties and treating patients from across India and more than 100 countries . With over 1,500 outpatient visits daily and more than 500 international patient consultations monthly, Apollo Hospitals, Greams Road has cemented its reputation as a preferred destination for advanced medical care .

Infrastructure and Facilities
The hospital campus, located at 21, Greams Road in the Thousand Lights area of Chennai, combines strategic accessibility with state-of-the-art medical infrastructure . The facility operates round-the-clock, providing comprehensive healthcare services including emergency medicine, intensive care, advanced diagnostics, and specialized surgical procedures. The hospital's infrastructure supports a full spectrum of care, from outpatient consultations and preventive health checkups to complex multi-organ transplants and robotic-assisted surgeries .

The Greams Road campus has evolved into an integrated healthcare ecosystem, most recently expanding to include Apollo AyurVAID Hospitals, a flagship 35-bed precision Ayurveda facility inaugurated in November 2025 . This expansion reflects the hospital's commitment to integrative healthcare, combining the precision of evidence-based Ayurveda with the scientific rigour of modern medicine . The AyurVAID facility, inaugurated by Dr. Prathap C. Reddy himself, offers inpatient and outpatient services for orthopaedic, neurological, neurodegenerative disorders, metabolic illnesses, women's health, paediatric developmental issues, sports injuries, and dermatological conditions .

Clinical Specialties and Medical Excellence
Cardiology and Cardiac Sciences
Cardiology remains a cornerstone of Apollo Hospitals' clinical excellence. The hospital's heart centre, located within the Greams Road campus, functions as a dedicated centre of excellence for cardiovascular care . Apollo Hospitals Chennai holds the distinction of pioneering several cardiac firsts in India, including the first open-heart surgeries and cardiac catheterization procedures . The institution offers comprehensive cardiac services including coronary angioplasty, rotablation, coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG), TAVI/TAVR procedures, pacemaker and ICD implantation, minimally invasive and robotic heart surgery, as well as heart transplantation and LVAD implantation .

The heart-lung transplant programme at Apollo Hospitals is globally recognized, offering comprehensive care for patients with advanced heart and lung disease . The hospital is one of the few centres in India capable of performing heart-lung, heart-liver, and heart-kidney multi-organ transplants . The transplant programme is backed by expert multidisciplinary teams, next-generation Extracorporeal Membrane Oxygenation (ECMO) systems, and comprehensive Mechanical Circulatory Support (MCS) options including Ventricular Assist Devices (VADs), Impella devices, and Total Artificial Heart (TAH) implantation . The hospital maintains 24/7 rapid response capabilities with dedicated land and air ambulance services, including ECMO support for patient transfers .

Oncology
Apollo Hospitals Chennai has been at the forefront of cancer care in South Asia. The hospital established South Asia's first Proton Cancer Centre, marking a significant milestone in advanced radiation therapy . The oncology programme encompasses comprehensive cancer diagnosis and treatment using chemotherapy, radiation therapy, targeted treatments, and immunotherapy . Surgical oncology services include both conventional and robotic cancer surgeries, while the bone marrow transplant programme provides advanced treatment options for haematological malignancies . The hospital's approach to oncology integrates supportive care and early detection strategies, ensuring comprehensive patient management from diagnosis through treatment and recovery .

Neurosciences
The neurology and neurosurgery departments at Apollo Hospitals provide comprehensive care for disorders of the brain, spinal cord, and nervous system . Clinical services address conditions including stroke, epilepsy, Parkinson's disease, migraine, and neurodegenerative disorders. Advanced surgical interventions include cerebrovascular and neuroendovascular surgeries, minimally invasive spine surgery, epilepsy surgery, deep brain stimulation (DBS), and decompressive craniectomy for stroke management . The department's multidisciplinary approach combines advanced diagnostics with cutting-edge therapeutic interventions.

Robotic Surgery
Apollo Hospitals, Greams Road has established itself as a leader in robotic-assisted surgery across multiple specialties. The robotic ENT Head & Neck Surgery programme offers cutting-edge care using the latest robotic technology, providing enhanced precision, minimal scarring, reduced postoperative pain, and faster recovery times . Conditions treated through robotic ENT surgery include head and neck cancers, cancers of the tonsils, base of tongue, larynx, hypopharynx, thyroid nodules, parathyroid tumours, salivary gland tumours, and various paediatric conditions . The robotic surgery programme extends to other specialties including gastrointestinal surgery, gynaecology, urology, and orthopaedics, with robotic joint replacement surgeries for knee and hip procedures .

Gastroenterology and Hepatology
The gastroenterology and hepatology department manages disorders of the digestive system and liver, including ulcers, hepatitis, liver diseases, and gastrointestinal cancers . Advanced endoscopic procedures include upper gastrointestinal endoscopy, colonoscopy, capsule endoscopy, manometry, EUS-guided biopsy and drainage procedures, POEM and third-space endoscopy, and endoscopic resection procedures including ESD and EMR . Laparoscopic gastrointestinal surgeries including cholecystectomy, appendectomy, and hernia repair are performed with minimally invasive techniques .

Nephrology and Urology
The nephrology department addresses kidney-related disorders including chronic kidney disease, dialysis management, electrolyte imbalances, and hypertension affecting kidney function . Dialysis services include haemodialysis, peritoneal dialysis, and continuous renal replacement therapy (CRRT). Urology services cover urinary tract conditions and male reproductive system disorders, with procedures including prostate surgery (TURP and laser surgery), lithotripsy for kidney stones, laparoscopic and robotic urological surgeries, and treatment for urinary incontinence .

Transplantation Services
Apollo Hospitals, Greams Road has earned international recognition for its transplantation programme, offering kidney, liver, heart, lung, and multi-organ transplants . The kidney transplant programme includes both living and cadaveric donors, while liver transplantation serves both adult and paediatric patients . The hospital is one of the few centres in India offering combined heart-lung, heart-liver, and heart-kidney transplants . The transplant programme is supported by a multidisciplinary team including transplant surgeons, anaesthetists, critical care specialists, and transplant coordinators, ensuring comprehensive evaluation, surgical expertise, and post-transplant care .

Orthopaedics and Musculoskeletal Care
The orthopaedics department specializes in treating bone, joint, and musculoskeletal disorders including fractures, arthritis, sports injuries, and joint replacement surgeries . The hospital offers both conventional and robotic-assisted joint replacement for knee and hip procedures. Arthroscopy and ligament reconstruction (ACL/PCL/MCL) procedures are performed using minimally invasive techniques. Spine surgeries, including scoliosis correction, limb lengthening and deformity correction, and diabetic foot surgery are also offered .

Integrative and Complementary Medicine
The recent addition of Apollo AyurVAID Hospitals at Greams Road represents a significant step in integrative healthcare . This 35-bed precision Ayurveda facility offers authentic Kerala Ayurveda treatments for chronic diseases, rehabilitation, palliative care, and end-of-life care . The facility provides all types of Panchakarma treatments including Vamana, Virechana, Nasya, Vasti, and Raktamokshana, along with classical Kerala external therapies like shirodhara, pizchil, and njavara kizhi . The integrative care model addresses orthopaedic disorders including arthritis, spondylitis, and back pain; neurological diseases including Parkinson's, stroke rehabilitation, and road traffic accident recovery; endocrinological imbalances including Type 2 diabetes and thyroid conditions; integrative oncology including cancer rehabilitation and chemotherapy side effect management; and gastrointestinal disorders including IBS, IBD, and ulcerative colitis .

The AyurVAID facility emphasizes evidence-based, clinically governed precision Ayurveda designed to complement modern medicine . The 'Tested Safe' AyurVAID range of classical Ayurveda medicines, medical foods, and OTC formulations undergo rigorous testing, with safety reports accessible via QR codes for complete transparency . The facility offers both inpatient and outpatient services with consultation timings from 9:00 am to 5:00 pm, seven days a week .

Emergency and Critical Care Services
Apollo Hospitals operates round-the-clock emergency and trauma care services supported by modern intensive care units and advanced imaging facilities . The emergency department is equipped to handle medical emergencies, trauma cases, cardiac emergencies, stroke, and critical care admissions. The hospital maintains dedicated ICUs for cardiac care, neurosciences, medical and surgical critical care, and neonatal and paediatric intensive care. Advanced life support systems, mechanical ventilation, and continuous monitoring capabilities ensure comprehensive critical care delivery.

Patient Care and International Services
Apollo Hospitals, Greams Road is renowned for its patient-centric approach, combining clinical excellence with compassionate care . The hospital caters to a diverse patient population, including international patients from over 100 countries. International patient services include dedicated coordinators, visa assistance, travel arrangements, accommodation support, and language interpretation services. The hospital's reputation for world-class care at affordable costs has made it a preferred destination for medical tourism.

Medical Education and Training
Apollo Hospitals Group operates teaching facilities including Apollo University in Chittoor, Andhra Pradesh, and Apollo Buckingham Health Sciences Campus in the United Kingdom . The Greams Road campus serves as a training ground for medical professionals across various specialties, contributing to the development of healthcare talent in India and globally. The hospital maintains academic affiliations and conducts continuing medical education programmes, workshops, and training sessions for healthcare professionals.

Research and Innovation
Apollo Hospitals has consistently been at the forefront of medical innovation in India. The institution has introduced numerous medical firsts, including pioneering complex cardiac procedures, establishing advanced robotic surgery programmes, and launching South Asia's first Proton Cancer Centre . The hospital continues to invest in research and development, clinical trials, and the adoption of emerging technologies to improve patient outcomes and advance medical knowledge.

Quality and Accreditation
Apollo Hospitals, Greams Road maintains rigorous quality standards across all clinical and operational areas. The hospital is accredited by the National Accreditation Board for Hospitals and Healthcare Providers (NABH) and other international quality organizations. Quality initiatives include clinical protocols, patient safety programmes, infection control measures, and continuous quality improvement processes.

Social Responsibility and Community Health
True to its founding ethos of serving all regardless of ability to pay, Apollo Hospitals undertakes various community health initiatives. These include health awareness programmes, preventive health checkup camps, disease screening, and health education initiatives. The hospital also participates in government health programmes and provides subsidized care for economically disadvantaged patients.

Future Directions
Apollo Hospitals continues to evolve, embracing new technologies and treatment modalities. The expansion of the AyurVAID facility represents a commitment to integrative healthcare, combining traditional wisdom with modern medical science . The hospital continues to invest in robotic surgery, artificial intelligence in healthcare, telemedicine, and digital health solutions. International expansion and partnerships with healthcare providers in Uzbekistan, Bangladesh, and the United Kingdom reflect the group's global ambitions .

Conclusion
Apollo Hospitals, Greams Road, Chennai, stands as a testament to the vision of Dr. Prathap C. Reddy and the transformative power of private healthcare in India. From its founding in 1983 as a 150-bed hospital offering advanced cardiac care at affordable prices, it has grown into a comprehensive healthcare institution serving patients from across the globe . The hospital combines over 60 medical specialties, advanced technology, robotic surgery, transplantation services, and integrative medicine with a patient-centric approach . The recent addition of the precision Ayurveda facility reflects the institution's commitment to holistic, evidence-based care that addresses the root causes of disease and supports prevention, recovery, and sustained wellbeing . As Apollo Hospitals continues to expand and innovate, the Greams Road campus remains the beating heart of the group, embodying its mission of making world-class healthcare accessible and affordable for all.
